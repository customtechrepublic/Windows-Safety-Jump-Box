# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Custom PC Republic Windows Safety Jump Box community is dedicated to creating a **welcoming, inclusive, and professional environment** for all contributors, users, and community members, regardless of:

- Age, body size, or disability
- Ethnicity, national origin, or immigration status
- Gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation
- Experience level or technical background
- Religion, political beliefs, or ideology
- Geographic location or time zone
- Professional background or industry

We are committed to providing a harassment-free community experience for everyone and to fostering a culture of mutual respect and psychological safety.

---

## Expected Behavior

### Be Professional & Respectful

- ✅ Treat all community members with dignity and respect
- ✅ Assume good intentions and allow room for learning
- ✅ Use welcoming and inclusive language
- ✅ Listen actively and thoughtfully to others' perspectives
- ✅ Acknowledge your mistakes and learn from feedback

### Be Collaborative

- ✅ Work constructively toward shared goals
- ✅ Share knowledge freely and help others learn
- ✅ Give credit appropriately for others' work
- ✅ Provide constructive feedback with actionable suggestions
- ✅ Support diversity of thought and approach

### Be Honest & Transparent

- ✅ Be truthful in all communications
- ✅ Clearly state your limitations or conflicts of interest
- ✅ Acknowledge when you don't know something
- ✅ Be transparent about affiliations and motivations
- ✅ Document and communicate decisions clearly

### Keep the Community Safe

- ✅ Report concerning behavior to maintainers
- ✅ Respect privacy and confidential information
- ✅ Follow responsible disclosure practices for security
- ✅ Don't spam or promote products inappropriately
- ✅ Respect intellectual property and licenses

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

We **do not tolerate** any of the following:

### Harassment & Discrimination

- ❌ Hateful comments targeting protected characteristics
- ❌ Deliberate intimidation or threats
- ❌ Sexual harassment or inappropriate advances
- ❌ Targeted attacks on someone's character or competence
- ❌ Mockery or ridicule based on identity
- ❌ Exclusion or marginalization based on background

### Disruptive Behavior

- ❌ Derailing discussions or off-topic trolling
- ❌ Repeated violations of community guidelines
- ❌ Spamming or promotion of unrelated products/services
- ❌ Abusive language or personal attacks
- ❌ Attempting to flood channels or overwhelm discussions
- ❌ Bad faith arguments or bad-faith participation

### Inappropriate Content

- ❌ Sexually explicit or violent content
- ❌ Illegal content or discussions
- ❌ Promotion of illegal activities
- ❌ Distribution of malware or harmful code (outside of security research context)
- ❌ Doxxing or sharing private information without consent
- ❌ Unsolicited promotion or advertising

### Policy Violations

- ❌ Violation of others' privacy
- ❌ Disclosure of confidential information
- ❌ Unauthorized use of others' credentials or accounts
- ❌ Attempting to bypass security measures
- ❌ Violation of applicable laws or regulations

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience a violation of this Code of Conduct:

#### For Immediate Threats or Harassment

1. **Do not engage** with the person
2. **Document** the behavior (screenshots, links, timestamps)
3. **Report immediately** to: `conduct@custompc.local`
4. **Contact GitHub Support** (for platform violations)

#### For Other Violations

1. **Report to maintainers:** `conduct@custompc.local`
2. **Include:** Description, links, context, and your contact info
3. **Allow time** for investigation
4. **Maintain confidentiality**

### Report Template

```
Subject: [CODE OF CONDUCT REPORT] {Brief Summary}

Reporter Information:
- Name/Handle: [Your name or handle]
- Contact: [Email or preferred contact method]
- Confidentiality: [Do you want your identity kept confidential? Yes/No]

Incident Information:
- Date/Time: [When did it occur]
- Location: [GitHub issue #, PR #, discussion link, etc.]
- Participants: [Who was involved]

Description:
[Detailed description of the incident, what was said/done, why it violates CoC]

Supporting Evidence:
[Links, screenshots, or other evidence]

Impact:
[How did this affect you or the community]

Resolution Desired:
[What action would you like taken]
```

### Investigation & Response

1. **Acknowledgment:** Report received within 48 hours
2. **Investigation:** Conducted within 5-7 business days
3. **Response:** Community member notified of findings
4. **Action:** Appropriate response taken (see below)
5. **Appeal:** Reporter may appeal within 14 days

### Possible Responses

Depending on severity and context:

#### Warning & Education

- ❌ First-time minor violations
- **Response:** Private communication explaining violation and expectations

#### Temporary Suspension

- ❌ Repeated violations or moderate violations
- **Response:** 7-30 day suspension from community spaces
- **Conditions:** May be lifted with demonstrated commitment to CoC

#### Permanent Ban

- ❌ Severe, repeated, or criminal behavior
- **Response:** Immediate removal from all community spaces
- **Note:** Appeals possible with evidence of change (after 6+ months minimum)

---

## Appeals Process

If you believe an enforcement action was unjust:

1. **Submit Appeal:** Email `conduct@custompc.local` within 30 days
2. **Include:**
   - Your name and reference number
   - Why you believe the action was incorrect
   - New evidence (if applicable)
   - Your proposed resolution

3. **Review:** Independent reviewer will assess (different from original reporter)
4. **Decision:** Within 14 days
5. **Final:** Decision is final; additional appeals not accepted

## Additional Guidelines

### Constructive Disagreement

Disagreement is **healthy and encouraged**. Here's how to disagree respectfully:

✅ **DO:**
- Focus on ideas, not people
- Ask clarifying questions
- Acknowledge valid points
- Suggest alternatives
- Be willing to change your mind

❌ **DON'T:**
- Attack the person
- Make assumptions
- Dismiss ideas without consideration
- Bring up past conflicts
- Yell or use CAPS

### Debate vs. Argument

**Healthy Debate:**
- Exchange of ideas with goal of mutual understanding
- Focus on facts and evidence
- Respectful tone even when disagreeing
- Goal: Make the best decision

**Unhealthy Arguments:**
- Attempts to "win" or prove the other wrong
- Emotional escalation
- Personal attacks or defensiveness
- Goal: Validate personal position

### Feedback Principles

**Giving Feedback:**
- ✅ Be specific and actionable
- ✅ Focus on behavior, not character
- ✅ Ask if they want feedback first (sometimes)
- ✅ Assume good intentions
- ✅ Offer solutions or alternatives

**Receiving Feedback:**
- ✅ Thank the person for the feedback
- ✅ Listen without immediately defending
- ✅ Ask clarifying questions
- ✅ Take time to consider
- ✅ Respond thoughtfully

## Resources & Support

### If You're Being Harassed

1. **Leave the situation** if you feel unsafe
2. **Report to maintainers** immediately
3. **Contact GitHub** (if on GitHub platform)
4. **Reach out to others** for support
5. **Take care of yourself**

### If You've Violated the CoC

1. **Acknowledge the violation**
2. **Apologize sincerely**
3. **Listen to feedback**
4. **Make amends where appropriate**
5. **Commit to change**

We believe in accountability AND redemption. If you've made a mistake, we welcome your growth and learning.

### Support Resources

- **Crisis Support:** SAMHSA National Helpline 1-800-662-4357
- **Harassment/Discrimination:** Local civil rights organizations
- **Mental Health:** Psychology Today therapist finder
- **Workplace Issues:** EEOC (Equal Employment Opportunity Commission)
